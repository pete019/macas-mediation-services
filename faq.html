Frequently Asked Questions

What is mediation?

Mediation is a process where a neutral third party (mediator) helps disputing parties communicate, understand each other's perspectives, and work toward a mutually acceptable resolution. Unlike court proceedings, mediation is confidential, flexible, and focuses on finding solutions that work for everyone.

How is mediation different from litigation?

Mediation is faster, more cost-effective, and maintains confidentiality. It allows parties to control the outcome rather than having a judge decide. Mediation preserves relationships better than adversarial court proceedings and often leads to more satisfactory long-term solutions.

How long does mediation take?

Most mediations can be resolved within 2-4 sessions, typically completed within 2-8 weeks. The duration depends on the complexity of the dispute and the willingness of parties to cooperate. Some simple cases may be resolved in a single session.

How much does mediation cost?

Our mediation fees are significantly lower than court litigation. We offer flexible payment options and transparent pricing. Initial consultations are available to discuss your situation and provide an accurate quote based on the complexity of your case.

Is mediation confidential?

Yes. Everything discussed during mediation is strictly confidential and cannot be used in court proceedings. This confidentiality encourages open communication and honest discussion between parties.

What if we cannot reach an agreement?

If parties cannot reach an agreement through mediation, they still retain the right to pursue legal action in court. However, our experience shows that most parties find acceptable solutions through our mediation process.

Are mediators qualified and certified?

Yes. All our mediators are professionally trained and certified in conflict resolution and mediation. They have extensive experience handling various types of disputes across employment, family, and property sectors.

Can mediation handle complex disputes?

Absolutely. Our mediators are experienced in handling complex disputes involving multiple parties, substantial financial issues, and sensitive personal matters. We have the expertise to guide complex cases toward resolution.

What types of disputes do you handle?

We specialize in employment disputes, family matters, and land/property disputes. These include wrongful dismissal, workplace conflicts, child custody, inheritance issues, boundary disputes, evictions, and many others.
